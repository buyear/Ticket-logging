Ticket Types

Two tickets.
Different purposes.

Understanding the difference between an Incident and a Problem ticket is the foundation of effective ITSM.

🚨

Incident Ticket

An unplanned disruption or degradation of an IT service. The goal is to restore normal service operation as quickly as possible and minimise the impact on the business — not necessarily to find the root cause.

Reactive · Service Restore
INC
🔍

Problem Ticket

The underlying cause of one or more incidents. Problem Management proactively identifies the root cause, documents a known error, and eliminates the issue permanently to prevent future recurrences.

Proactive · Root Cause
PRB
⚡

Major Incident

A high-impact incident that significantly disrupts critical business services. Requires a dedicated bridge call, major incident manager, and post-incident review. Automatically triggers a linked Problem record.

Critical · Escalated
MJR
📋

Known Error

A problem that has been analysed and has either a root cause or a documented workaround. Known Errors sit in the Known Error Database (KEDB) and help technicians resolve incidents faster while a permanent fix is in progress.

Documented · KEDB
KE
